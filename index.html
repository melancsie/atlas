Neurodivergence

These are not diagnoses. They are lenses. They explain the wiring, not what's wrong with it.

ADHD · Strong signal
multiple simultaneous thoughts constant background music many tabs open systems that don't stick impulsive, sends mid-thought deep rule-aversion interest-driven attention

The chaotic inner world isn't noise — it's high-velocity parallel processing. The inability to maintain systems isn't weakness, it's a mismatch between structure and how this brain actually runs.

Autism (likely masking) · Moderate signal
huge variance in how others see you inner world invisible to others dislikes intense scrutiny pattern recognition as core strength context-dependent functioning follows rules only when watched multi-modal sensory inner experience

AuDHD (the ADHD + autism overlap) is chronically underdiagnosed in women, who mask early and effectively. The "flat personality / interesting" split is classic masking variance.

What the wiring explains
The multi-modal mind
Verbal, visual, feeling, and sound thoughts running simultaneously isn't unusual — it's how this brain processes. Multiple channels, not one.
The invisible interior
A very full inner world that almost nobody accesses is a feature of both ADHD (self-absorbed in thought) and autism masking (deliberate internal/external split).
The performance gap
Functioning varies dramatically with conditions (cycle, energy, confidence, environment). This isn't inconsistency — it's a high-sensitivity system responding to inputs.

State System

Named states help you identify where you are and navigate better. These are early drafts — refine them as you learn yourself more.

Rio Mode
Clear skies · Full presence
Embodied, confident, socially alive. Body feels right. Environment is warm and low-judgment. Conversation flows. Intelligence and charm both accessible. The most integrated version of self — Taurus sun fully expressed.
Conditions: body confidence, aesthetic environment, people on same wavelength, warmth, absence of harsh scrutiny
Glass House
Overcast · High social anxiety
Being watched too carefully. Performing compliance. Hyper-aware of judgment. Gemini rising working overtime to present an acceptable surface while the interior goes quiet and guarded. The "flat personality" others sometimes see.
Triggers: authority figures, unfamiliar social settings, being the centre of attention, perceived judgment
The Spiral
Electrical storm · Internal
Hyper-analysis activated. Thoughts multiplying. Worries compounding. The inner world gets very loud and the outer world goes very quiet. Withdrawal. Hard to access the socially capable self. Can last hours or days.
Triggers: something goes wrong, rejection or perceived rejection, body dissatisfaction, overstimulation
Flow State
Still and charged
The ADHD brain locked onto something interesting. All tabs converge on one problem. Time disappears. The pattern-connecting and outside-the-box thinking are fully online. Rare but the most productive and satisfying state.
Conditions: genuine intellectual or creative interest, no performance pressure, right energy level
Cycle influence

Your cycle significantly affects which state is accessible. This isn't weakness — it's high biological sensitivity. Mapping which phase of your cycle correlates with which state would give you powerful predictive data. Consider logging this in your Insight Inbox.

Origins & Wounds

Not wallowing — archaeology. Why do certain traits exist? Tracing them back turns description into understanding. This section grows slowly and with honesty.

Hypothesis
The Invisible Interior
Almost nobody knows what's actually happening inside you. This could be innate (neurodivergent wiring that makes masking automatic), or it could also be learned — an environment where showing the interior felt unsafe or unwelcome, so you stopped. Worth exploring: was there a person, or a period, where being seen felt dangerous?
Hypothesis
Body as Conditional Self-Worth
When physical appearance is an anchor for identity, something installed that connection early. Taurus sun explains some of it — the body genuinely matters to this placement. But the conditionality ("I am only at my best when...") suggests a wound too. What messages did you receive about your body, and from whom?
Hypothesis
Rule-Following Under Scrutiny
You hate rules by instinct — but follow them when being watched. This gap between private self and public compliance suggests the cost of breaking rules was at some point high enough to install an anxiety response. Social anxiety as a learned adaptation rather than a fixed trait.

The Shadow

The parts of yourself you suppress, disown, or haven't acknowledged yet. Jung called this the shadow — not the evil self, but the unintegrated self. What you most judge in others is often a clue to what you haven't accepted in yourself. This section demands the most honesty and grows the most slowly.

Shadow prompts — for reflection
Prompt 1
What quality do you most dislike in other people? It probably lives in you too, in a form you haven't admitted.
Prompt 2
What do you secretly want that you'd be embarrassed to admit? Not in terms of desires — in terms of who you want to be.
Prompt 3
What version of yourself are you most afraid other people will see?
